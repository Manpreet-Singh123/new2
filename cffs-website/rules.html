Rules

  1. In order to register for CFFS, go to the registration page (LINK). You will be required to provide the name of your team and team leader along with a short abstract of the idea or problem that you intend to solve. You don't need your entire team ready immediately. Addition of members is allowed uptil a month before the judging commences (tentative DATE).
  2. Create a repository for your team on github and transfer its ownership to IISERM.
  3. Once the ownership transfer has been accepted by us, you can create a fork of this repository and can start making a website of your team inside this repository. The website should contain information about the idea your team is working on. At the end of a month, you will be accepted to send a Pull Request (PR) to the main repository on github. This PR should contain the website you have made. It must also have a page for your team listing the names and roles of team members (additions are allowed till a month before judging commences). The PR should also contain the progress you have made in the first month, it could include, say, a subsidiary problem that you have solved, etc.
  4. You need to send a PR every month till the final submission (due by DATE) detailing all that has been achieved by the team during the month. However, you are allowed to take a month off, the choice of the month is up-to the team. Do note, however, that the submission of the PR of the first month is compulsory.
  5. By the end of the contest, you need to make a complete Wiki of your project and put everything that you have done on your webpage. Any code that you have written should be added to a separate folder within the same github repo and relevant pieces of code may be linked on the website, if required. The Wiki will form an important part of the judging, so make sure that the Wiki at the end contains everything that you wish to highlight. The monthly PRs are just meant to keep a track of your progress and judge the consistency of the team.
  6. Your Team Wiki is how you and your teammates will communicate your entire project to the judges. So make sure that the wiki at the end contains everything that you wish to highlight. Don't wait for the last moment to finish your wiki.
  7. Remember that you need to keep the information about your progress over the period of the contest intact. Changes made to this information will be considered cheating. The monthly PRs are meant to track your progress and judge your consistency. In case it is found that there is a discrepancy between your monthly PRs and the final wiki, your team shall be disqualified.
  8. Make sure that you add all the attribution to your wiki. Using other people's work without proper citation will be considered plagiarism and penalized. In severe cases, it could lead to disqualification, depending on the discretion of the judges.
  9. Your team will be required to make a video presentation(maximum time limit 30 min) and submit it before the wiki-freeze, which will happen at the end of July. No PRs or submissions shall be accepted after 31st July, 23:59(IST).
  10. You will be required to attend a judging session where the judges will ask you questions on your work. The dates will be announced soon.
